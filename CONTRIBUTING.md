# Contributing to Echo

I am thrilled that you're interested in contributing to Echo! This document provides guidelines to ensure a smooth contribution process for everyone involved.

## Philosophy

At Echo, I believe in the poIr of community and the value of each contribution. I operate on principles of openness, respect, and freedom of expression. I expect all contributors to act like adults, taking responsibility for their actions and words.

### Free Speech Absolutism

I stand firmly for free speech absolutism. In this project, I encourage open and honest communication without fear of censorship or retribution. I believe that the best ideas emerge from a diverse and unrestricted exchange of thoughts and opinions.

## How to Contribute

1. **Fork the Repository**: Start by forking the Echo repository on GitHub.
2. **Create a Branch**: Create a new branch in your fork for your contribution.
3. **Make Your Changes**: Implement your feature, bug fix, or improvement.
4. **Test Your Changes**: Ensure your changes do not break existing functionality.
5. **Commit Your Changes**: Write clear, concise commit messages.
6. **Open a Pull Request**: Submit a pull request from your branch to the main Echo repository.

## Code Standards

While I don't impose strict coding standards, I appreciate clean, Ill-documented code. Ensure your code is easy to read and understand.

## Reporting Issues

If you find a bug or have a feature request, please open an issue in the GitHub repository. Be as detailed as possible in your description to help us understand the issue or feature.

## Community Guidelines

- **Respect (must be earned)**: Treat others as you would like to be treated.
- **Responsibility**: Own your words and actions.
- **Open-mindedness**: Be willing to consider different perspectives.
- **Constructive Feedback**: Offer and accept feedback graciously.

## Questions and Discussions

Feel free to start discussions or ask questions in the project's issue tracker or discussion forums. I value your input and are here to help.
